Ratatouille Bruschetta

Servings: 6 servings Source: my.forksmealplanner.com

Ingredients

15 oz eggplants

diced (≈6 cups)

1 1/2 zucchini

quartered lengthwise & sliced (≈3 cups)

1 1/2 yellow onions

chopped (≈1 1/2 cups)

9 cloves garlic

minced (≈3 tablespoons)

3/4 cup white wine

1 1/2 (15-oz) cans low-sodium fire-roasted diced tomatoes

with their juices (≈2 1/4 cups)

6 oz roasted red bell peppers

1 tablespoon Italian seasoning

FOR SERVING

3 oz baby arugula

3 tablespoons balsamic vinegar

18 slices whole-grain baguettes

1 1/4 cups hummus

Directions

In a stockpot over medium heat, cook eggplant, zucchini, onions, and garlic, stirring occasionally, until tender, 5 to 7 minutes. Add white wine 1 to 2 tablespoons at a time as needed, to keep vegetables from sticking.

Add fire-roasted tomatoes with their juices, roasted peppers, remaining wine, and Italian seasoning to pot. Bring to a boil, uncovered. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer until most of the liquid has evaporated, about 5 minutes. Remove from heat. Let cool.

In a bowl, toss arugula with balsamic vinegar.

Toast baguette slices, then spread hummus on each slice. Top each with arugula, followed by ratatouille. Serve ratatouille chilled or at room temperature.

Notes

White Wine: If you’d like, you can substitute low-sodium vegetable broth for the wine.

Roasted Red Bell Pepper: Roasted bell peppers come in various forms; some are cut and stored in brine, while others are sliced and packed in oil. Make sure that the ones you use are oil-free.

Hummus: Be sure to check the label to ensure the hummus does not contain added oils. Look for Engine 2, Oasis, Roots, and Cedars.
