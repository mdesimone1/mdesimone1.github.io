Caribbean Chili

Servings: 4 servings Source: my.forksmealplanner.com

Ingredients

2/3 cup brown rice

2 (15-oz) cans black beans

drained & rinsed (≈3 cups)

10 oz frozen corn

(≈2 cups)

1/2 (32-fl oz) carton low-sodium vegetable broth

(≈2 cups)

2 tablespoons Jamaican jerk seasoning

1/2 cup fresh cilantro

sea salt

freshly ground black pepper

2 habanero chiles

Directions

Add

The herbs and spices in jerk seasoning add delicious island flavor, while habanero brings a bit of island heat to this chili dish.

Allow about 10 minutes extra to prepare the grains and steam the sweet potatoes if you are making this recipe from start to finish in one day.

In a saucepan over high heat, bring rice and 1 1/3 cups water to a boil. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 45 minutes. Remove from heat and let cool; fluff rice with a fork.

In a steamer insert set over boiling water, steam sweet potatoes, covered, until tender when pierced with a fork, about 10 minutes. Remove from heat and uncover pot to let cool.

In a stockpot over medium heat, cook onions, bell peppers, and garlic, stirring occasionally, until onions start to turn translucent, 3 to 4 minutes. Add water 1 to 2 tablespoons at a time as needed, to keep vegetables from sticking.

Add tomatoes with their juices, black beans, corn, vegetable broth, and jerk seasoning. Increase heat to high, cover, and bring to a boil. Add steamed sweet potatoes and 1/4 cup cilantro. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer until flavors meld, 5 minutes.

Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Scatter remaining cilantro and habanero over chili, if desired, and serve alongside brown rice.
