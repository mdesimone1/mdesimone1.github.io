Sweet Potato Nuggets

Servings: 4 servings Source: my.forksmealplanner.com

Ingredients

FOR THE SWEET POTATO NUGGETS:

2 sweet potatoes

1/2 cup brown rice

rinsed & drained (≈1 1/2 cups cooked)

1 (15-oz) can chickpeas

1 cup quick-cooking oats

1 cup shredded carrots

4 scallions

1/4 cup unflavored plant milk

sea salt

freshly ground black pepper

1 cup whole-wheat panko breadcrumbs

FOR THE BARBECUE SAUCE:

1 cup barbecue sauce

1/2 cup frozen peach slices

FOR THE STEAMED BROCCOLI

3 lbs fresh or frozen broccoli florets

freshly ground black pepper

2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice

Directions

Add

This recipe makes 48 nuggets.

One serving is 12 nuggets, so enjoy any leftovers throughout the week.

Talk about a happy meal—your little ones will love dipping these crispy panko-crusted bites into our tangy-sweet barbecue sauce.

Allow about 1 hour extra to bake the sweet potatoes and prepare the rice if you are making this recipe from start to finish in one day.

Preheat the oven to 350°F.

Use a fork to poke holes in sweet potatoes a few times. Place potatoes on a baking sheet and bake until tender, about 1 hour. Let cool enough to handle. (Alternatively, heat potatoes in the microwave for 8 to 10 minutes or until tender.)

In a saucepan over high heat, bring rice and 3/4 cup water to a boil. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 45 minutes. Remove from heat and let cool; fluff rice with a fork.

Increase oven temperature to 400°F. Line baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone baking mats.

Halve sweet potatoes and scoop out the flesh. Discard skins. Transfer sweet potato flesh, along with chickpeas, to a large bowl; coarsely mash. Add cooked rice, oats, carrots, scallions, and lemon pepper. Mix well, adding just enough plant milk so that mixture comes together.

Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Form mixture into 1¼-inch nuggets (you should end up with about 48 nuggets. Place panko bread crumbs in a shallow bowl. Roll nuggets in panko to coat and place on prepared baking sheets.

Bake nuggets until the outsides are lightly toasted and the nuggets are heated through, about 15 minutes.

Meanwhile, in a steamer insert set over boiling water, steam broccoli, covered, until tender when pierced with a fork, about 5 minutes. Remove from heat and transfer to a bowl to cool.

Season with pepper and lemon juice.

In a high-powered blender (or food processor), combine barbecue sauce and thawed peaches. Pulse until smooth.

Serve nuggets alongside dipping sauce and broccoli.
