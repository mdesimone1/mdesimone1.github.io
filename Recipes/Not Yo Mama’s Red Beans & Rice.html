Not Yo Mamaâ€™s Red Beans & Rice

Prep Time: 25 min Cook Time: 2 hr Servings: 6

Ingredients

1 pound red beans


2 teaspoons salt


1 1/2 teaspoons each: onion powder, dried basil


1 teaspoon each: garlic powder, dry mustard


1/2 teaspoon each: freshly ground black pepper, white pepper, dried oregano, cayenne


3 cups chopped onions


1 cup chopped green bell pepper


1/2 cup chopped celery


8 1/2 cups chicken stock or broth, (fat removed)


2 cups grape juice


4 cups cooked long-grain white rice

Description

Paul Prudhommes Fork In The Road

Directions

1. Day 1: Rinse beans, place in a medium bowl and add enough water to cover by 3 or 4 inches. Soak overnight in the refridgerator. As the beans absorb the water, they will more than double in volume.

2. Day 2: Combine the seasoning mix ingredients (salt, onion powder, basil, garlic powder, mustard, black pepper, white pepper, oregano and cayenne) in a small bowl. Drain and rinse the beans. Drain again and set aside.

3. Heat a heavy 5-quart pot, preferably non-stick, over high heat about 4 minutes. (If using a ligher pot, use lower heat.) Add 2 cups of the onions, the bell pepper, the celery and 1 tablspoon of the seasoning mix. Stir and cook until vegetables start to brown, about 5 minutes. Add 1/2 cup of the chicken stock, scrape the bottom of the pot to clear all the browned bits, and cook 3 minutes.

4. Add the remaining onions, stir, and cook 5 minutes. Add the drained beans, 5 cups of the stock, and the remaining seasoning mix. Stir and cook 45 minutes, scraping the bottom of the pot occasionally to check for sticking and reducing heat if mixture is boiling too rapidly.

5. Add 1 cup grape juice and continue to cook 25 minutes. Caution: At this point, the starches in the beans tart to break down and stocking will occur more often. It is therefore important to check and clear the bottom of the pot frequently. Add the remaining stock and grape juice, turn the heat to medium, and cook until the beans are tender, and the liquid is thick and begins to look creamy, about 30 to 35 minutes.

6. Serve over the hot rice.

Nutrition information per serving: 409 calories, 3 grams fat, 3 milligrams cholesterol, 81 grams carbohydrate, 1,200 milligrams sodium, 17 grams protein.
