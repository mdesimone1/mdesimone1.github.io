Grilled Tortilla & Corn Soup

Servings: 6 servings Source: my.forksmealplanner.com

Ingredients

1 1/2 yellow onions

(≈9 oz)

1 1/2 green bell peppers

(≈12 oz)

12 plum tomatoes

halved (≈2 1/4 lbs)

3 jalapeños

9 corn tortillas

2 1/4 (32-fl oz) cartons low-sodium vegetable broth

2 tablespoons mild chili powder

1 tablespoon ground cumin

6 ears of corn

1 1/2 (15-oz) cans pinto beans

drained & rinsed (≈2 1/4 cups)

3/4 cup fresh cilantro

sea salt

freshly ground black pepper

FOR THE TOFU SOUR CREAM:

3/4 (14-oz) package low-fat extra-firm silken tofu

2 1/4 teaspoons fresh lemon juice

(optional)

2 1/4 teaspoons red wine vinegar

(optional)

sea salt

(optional)

FOR SERVING

1 1/2 limes

Directions

If you are using the grill:

Preheat the grill to medium.

If you are using the broiler:

Preheat the broiler to high. Line baking sheets with aluminum foil.

In a food processor (or a high-powered blender), purée drained tofu, lemon juice, and red wine vinegar until smooth and creamy. Season with salt to taste. Chill in the refrigerator until ready to serve.

If you are using the grill:

On a covered grill over direct medium heat, place onion, bell pepper, plum tomatoes, and jalapeño, cut sides down. Grill, turning once, until vegetables are almost tender and grill marks have formed, 6 to 8 minutes.

If you are using the broiler:

On prepared baking sheets, place onion, bell pepper, plum tomatoes, and jalapeño, cut sides up. Broil 4 inches from heat, turning once, until vegetables are just tender and browned in places, 6 to 8 minutes.

If you are using the grill:

On the grill over direct medium heat, grill tortillas until crisp and browned, turning once, 2 to 3 minutes. Let cool for 2 to 3 minutes.

If you are using the broiler:

Place tortillas directly on the rack and broil, turning once, until crisp and browned, 1 to 2 minutes.

Roughly chop vegetables and 6 tortillas.

In a stockpot over high heat, bring vegetable broth, grilled vegetables, chopped tortillas, chili powder, and cumin to a boil. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer, stirring occasionally, until vegetables are very tender, 5 minutes.

Using an immersion blender, purée soup until smooth (or purée in batches in a high-powered blender or food processor until nearly smooth then return to stockpot; see tip).

Add corn kernels, pinto beans, and 1/2 cup cilantro to soup. Simmer until corn is tender, 3 to 4 minutes. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Chop or break remaining tortillas into thin strips or small pieces. Sprinkle over soup with remaining 3 tablespoons cilantro. Top with tofu sour cream, if desired, and serve with lime wedges for squeezing.

Notes

Corn: Working over a large bowl, use a knife to carefully slice kernels from corn.

Blending: When using a blender to purée hot liquids, you may want to cover the lid with a towel to reduce the possibility of coming into contact with hot liquid or steam.

Jalapeno: Though most of the chile’s heat comes from the seeds (remove seeds for less heat), the oils in the pepper can irritate skin. Some people like to wear gloves, but most can get away with simply washing their hands after handling. Avoid contact with sensitive areas, especially your eyes.
