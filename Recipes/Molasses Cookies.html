Molasses Cookies

Ingredients

1 egg

¾ cup dark brown sugar

½ cup coconut oil in liquid form

⅓ cup unsulphured molasses

2T vanilla

2t cinnamon

1t ground ginger

1t ground cloves

½t ground nutmeg

pinch salt

2c all purpose flour

1t baking soda

Directions

1. To the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment (or use large mixing bowl and handmixer), combine the egg, brown sugar, coconut oil, molasses, vanilla and beat on medium high speed until well mixed, smooth, and glossy: about four minutes.

2. Stop, scrape down the sides of the bowl, add the cinnamon, ginger, cloves, nutmeg, salt, and beat on medium high speed until combined and smooth, about one minute.

3. Stop, scrape down the sides of the bowl, and add the flour, baking soda, and mix until just combined, about one minute.

4. Using a medium 2 inch cookie scoop, form heaping 2 tablespoon mounds about 17. Place mounds on a large plate, cover with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least three hours, or up to five days, before baking. Until chilled, dough will be very soft, mushy, limp, and is not suitable for baking; it must be chilled so the coconut oil re-solidifies. Do not bake with warm dough because cookies will spread and bake thinner and flatter.

5. Preheat to 350°, line baking sheets with silpats, or spray with cooking spray; set aside.

6. Make cinnamon sugar coating. Combine cinnamon and sugar in a bowl and stir to mix.

7. Roll each ball of dough through the coating, liberally coating all sides. After all cookies have been coated. I like to go back and double dip each mound, to get an extra thick coating.

8. Please coated mounds on baking sheets, spaced at least 2 inches apart. Bake nine minutes or until edges have set and tops are just beginning to set, even if undercooked and soft center. Do not bake longer than nine minutes for soft cookies because they firm up as they cool; bake for 9 to 10 minutes if you like firmer cookies. Allow cookies to cool on baking sheets for about five minutes before removing and transferring to a rack to finish cooling.

9. Store cookies air tight at room temperature for up to one week, or in the freezer for up to four months. Alternatively, and baked cookie dough can be stored airtight in the refrigerator for up to five days or in the freezer for up to four months, so consider baking only as many cookies as desired and save the remaining dough to be baked in the future when desired. Do not roll cookies through cinnamon sugar mixture until you plan to bake them.
