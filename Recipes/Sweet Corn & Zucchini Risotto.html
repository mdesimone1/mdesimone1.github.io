Sweet Corn & Zucchini Risotto

Servings: 4 servings Source: my.forksmealplanner.com

Ingredients

2 cups short-grain brown rice

4 ears of corn

husks & silk removed (≈2 cups kernels)

1 (8-oz) package sliced button mushrooms

(≈3 cups)

1 yellow onion

chopped (≈1 cup)

6 cloves garlic

1/2 cup white wine

1/2 (32-fl oz) carton low-sodium vegetable broth

2 cups unflavored plant milk

2 teaspoons fresh thyme

1 zucchini

1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika

10 oz cherry tomatoes

1/4 cup fresh chives

sea salt

freshly ground black pepper

Directions

In a medium saucepan over high heat, combine rice with 3 cups water. Cover and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 20 minutes. Remove pan from heat. (Rice will not be done at this point). Do not drain.

Working over a large bowl, use a knife to carefully slice kernels from corn.

In a stockpot over medium heat, cook mushrooms, onions, and garlic, stirring occasionally, until vegetables are tender, 3 to 4 minutes. Add white wine 1 to 2 tablespoons at a time as needed, to keep vegetables from sticking.

Add partially cooked rice to pot and mix well.

In a medium saucepan over high heat, bring remaining white wine, vegetable broth, plant milk, and thyme to a boil. Reduce heat to a simmer. Add simmering liquid, ½ cup at a time, to rice mixture. Cook, stirring frequently, until liquid is absorbed, about 5 minutes per ½ cup addition of liquid.

Add corn, zucchini, and smoked paprika when most of the liquid has been added. Stir in cherry tomatoes and chives when all the liquid has been absorbed. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Serve immediately.

Notes

White Wine: If you’d like, you can substitute low-sodium vegetable broth for the wine.
